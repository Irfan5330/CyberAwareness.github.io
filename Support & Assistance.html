Support and Assistance

Here you'll find a list of hotlines, government agencies, and nonprofit organizations dedicated to helping individuals and businesses address cyber threats, report incidents, and access valuable resources. From reporting cybercrime to seeking advice on protecting your digital identity, these resources are here to support you every step of the way.

Explore the links below to learn more about each resource and how they can assist you in safeguarding your online presence. Remember, when it comes to cybersecurity, knowledge is your best defense. Don't hesitate to reach out for help whenever you need it.

Hotlines and Helplines

Action Fraud:

Website: Action Fraud (https://www.actionfraud.police.uk)

The UK's national reporting center for fraud and cybercrime. Victims can report incidents online or by calling the hotline.

Hotline: 0300 123 2040

National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) - Incident Response:

Website: NCSC Incident Response (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/)

Offers guidance and support for organizations experiencing cybersecurity incidents.

Hotline (for organizations): 0800 272 2424

Government Agencies (NCSC)

National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC):

Website: (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/information/report-incident)

Provides cybersecurity guidance, resources, and incident response for the UK

Cyber Aware:

Website: (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/cyberaware)

A UK government initiative providing advice for individuals and small businesses on how to protect against cyber threats (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/cyberaware)

Nonprofit Organizations:

Get Safe Online:

Website: (https://www.getsafeonline.org/)

Provides practical advice on how to protect yourself, your computers, and mobile devices against fraud, identity theft, viruses, and many other problems encountered online.

Cyber Security Information Sharing Partnership (CiSP):

Website: (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/information/cisp)

A joint industry and government initiative to share cyber threat and vulnerability information in order to increase overall situational awareness of the cyber threat landscape in the UK.
